about

I am a first year PhD student at Cornell University, studying theoretical computer science. I received my BSE (summa cum laude) in computer science from Princeton University, where I also received a certificate in PACM. My primary interests lie in graph theory, combinatorial optimization, and complexity theory.


experience

Before I started my PhD, I spent some time at Berkeley, where I studied approximation algorithms with Alexandre Bayen. As an undergraduate at Princeton, I worked with Robert Tarjan on various graph coloring problems. I've spent a few summers at Google, most recently working with the Search and Google Research teams.
